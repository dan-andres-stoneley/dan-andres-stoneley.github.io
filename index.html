Core skills
Physics

Throughout my career, I've scratch built physics engines for Acclaim, Sony, and Lucid. One focussed on simulating large quantities of debris and ragdolls for an indoor shooting game. Another was multi-platform and scalable, suppoting the Formula One franchise across a range of differently specced devices. The latest is specialised for simulating high-fidelity vehicle dynamics running asyncronously alongside the Unreal Engine on modern hardware.

I've also shipped games using PhysX, Havok, and Bullet physics engines. As part of my work on Motorstorm Pacific Rift, I collaborated with the Havok developers to upgrade and port Havok features to the PS3 SPU architecture. On Destruction All Stars, I made significant modifications and additions to both PhysX and the Unreal Engine to upgrade them with soft-body physics deformation features.

Vehicle Dynamics

Combining a deep understanding of vehicle mechanics, an obssession for racing gaming, and a lifelong passion for motorsport, I provide a complete vehicle development package. On many projects, I've not only programmed the vehicle simulations, but also tuned the car gameplay, assisted in the game’s design, and collaborated with vehicle artists and environment builders. I've built experience for all aspects of integrating vehicle gameplay and directing and supporting other disciplines effectively.

I've developed a wide array of vehicle types, everything from cars, tanks, and monster trucks to flying craft. I've also created a broad spectrum of vehicle gameplay experiences such as arcade street racers, car combat games, and even a vehicle MOBA. My current passion project is the development of a next-generation simulation racing model to rival and surpass the likes of Gran Turismo and Asseto Corsa.

Destruction

Who doesn't love smashing their Lego after building it? Destruction is a complex problem requiring the coordination of varied technical approaches and disciplines. There are many tricks to selling the illusion, and a multitude of pitfalls to avoid. Fortunately, I've had many opportunities to smash Lego during my career and I've built a large toolbox of destruction solutions and techniques, an intuition for navigating the problem, and experience in directing disciplines to acheive high quality results.

Across various titles, I’ve implemented and coordinated solutions such as panel hinging and detachment systems, scratch layers, mesh morphing, skeletal manipulation, mass debris systems, soft-body physics deformation, vehicle crash and roll dynamics, building collapse, glass smashing, explosions, and much more.
